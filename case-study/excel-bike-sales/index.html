Case Study: Excel Bike Sales Dashboard

Excel | Pivot Tables | Slicers | Data Visualization

1. Project Overview

This case study explains how I built an Excel dashboard to analyze customer demographics, income levels, commute distances, and purchasing behavior. Using only Excel tools, I created a dynamic dashboard driven by PivotTables and slicers.

2. Dashboard – Full View

3. Dataset & Preparation

The dataset includes customer attributes such as:

  • Gender, Age, Marital Status
  • Occupation, Region, Income
  • Commute Distance
  • Whether they purchased a bike

Steps taken before analysis:

  • Cleaned missing and inconsistent categorical values
  • Created age groups and income brackets
  • Standardized region and occupation fields

4. Dashboard Foundations (Pivot Tables)

5. Major Insights Found

  • Married customers show significantly higher bike purchase rates.
  • Customers with shorter commute distance are more likely to buy a bike.
  • Higher income levels correlate with increased likelihood of purchase.
  • Customers aged 31–45 contribute to the highest sales segment.
  • Skilled manual and clerical occupations drive most purchases.

6. Techniques Used

  • PivotTables for segmentation and aggregation
  • PivotCharts for visualization
  • Slicer-based filtering for dynamic interaction
  • Data validation and conditional formatting

7. Final Outcome

This Excel dashboard demonstrates how powerful insights can be generated using Excel alone, especially when working with demographic and sales-based datasets. It provides business teams with instant visual insights to support marketing and sales decisions.
