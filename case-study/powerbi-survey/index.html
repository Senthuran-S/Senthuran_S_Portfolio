Case Study: Power BI Survey Data Dashboard

Data Analytics | Visualization | Power BI | Survey Insights

1. Project Overview

This case study explains how I transformed a raw data-professional survey dataset into a fully interactive, insight-driven Power BI dashboard. The dashboard helps users explore salary trends, job satisfaction, difficulty of breaking into tech roles, country-wise participation, and tool preferences across hundreds of respondents.

2. Dashboard – Full View

3. Dataset & Preparation

The dataset contains survey responses from global data professionals. Before visualization, I performed data cleaning in Power Query:

  • Removed duplicates and invalid entries
  • Standardized categorical values (job titles, countries, tools)
  • Converted salary ranges into numeric values
  • Created new “Seniority Level” and “Salary Group” fields
  • Handled missing values for satisfaction and work-life balance scores

4. Dashboard Components

4.1 Demographics & Role Breakdown

This section helps identify who participated in the survey and how roles are distributed across countries, industries, and career stages.

4.2 Key Insights Section

The insights section highlights real-world patterns found in the dataset.

5. Major Insights Identified

  • Data Scientists earned the highest average salaries among all surveyed roles.
  • Most survey participants are from the U.S., India, and the U.K.
  • Breaking into the data field is moderately challenging for most respondents.
  • Work-life balance satisfaction remains above average across all experience levels.
  • Python is the most preferred programming language by a wide margin.
  • Salary strongly correlates with total experience and seniority.

6. Techniques Used

  • Power Query for cleaning & shaping the data
  • DAX Measures (Avg Salary, Satisfaction Score, Seniority Buckets)
  • Dynamic visuals: KPIs, Donut Charts, Bar Charts, Maps
  • Filters for Country, Job Role, Industry, and Experience Level

7. Final Outcome

The final dashboard enables clear, dynamic exploration of global data-professional trends and allows for real-time segmentation across demographics, salary groups, and satisfaction levels.
